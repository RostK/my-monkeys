## Codebase Patterns

- 2026-07-14 — `tagsFor()` reads `fm.tags || fm.keywords`, so a frontmatter key literally named `keywords` is **already consumed as an artifact's tags** and will silently pollute the tag facet. Any new "search keywords" concept must use a different key (or live outside frontmatter entirely) or it will show up as clickable `#tags` in the sidebar and on cards. Evidence: `preview/scripts/build-index.mjs:81`.
- 2026-07-14 — `src/catalog.json` is **generated and gitignored**, and it is `import`ed (bundled into the JS) rather than `fetch`ed at runtime. Two consequences: you cannot hand-author data into it (re-run `npm run index` instead), and there is **no runtime "catalog failed to load" path** to design for — a bad catalog is a build failure, not a runtime one. Evidence: `preview/.gitignore:7`, `preview/src/data.js:8`.
- 2026-07-14 — CI grants `permissions: contents: read` and references **no secrets** in either workflow, so a build-time network/LLM call is not possible today without first adding an API-key secret. Any "generate X at build time" idea must either stay offline-and-committed or budget for that CI change. Evidence: `.github/workflows/preview-build.yml:26`, `.github/workflows/pages.yml:17`.

## Decisions

- 2026-07-14 — Chose **MiniSearch over Fuse.js** for client-side search. The deciding factor is that Fuse.js does **not** rank by relevance in the IR sense at all (see Tool & Library Notes), while MiniSearch implements real BM25+. MiniSearch is also the smaller bundle (5,814 B gzipped vs 9,266 B). Rejected: on-device embeddings / vector search — at 29 artifacts the vocabulary gap they'd close is closable far more cheaply by baking synonyms into the index. Evidence: `specs/preview/SPEC-01-2026-07-14-lexical-search-and-keyword-index.md`.
- 2026-07-14 — Search `keywords` are a **separate field from `tags`**, not a replacement, because the two have different jobs: `tags` are few (capped at 4), visible, and clickable (sidebar facet, `?tag=` URL param, card badge), whereas `keywords` are many (~10–20/artifact), invisible, and ranking-only. Merging them makes the tag facet unusable (~40 chips → ~400). Keywords are generated by an LLM **offline, once, and committed** — not at build time — because CI has no secrets and committed keywords stay human-reviewable and keep builds deterministic. Evidence: `preview/scripts/build-index.mjs:89`, `preview/src/components/Filters.jsx:67`, `preview/src/components/Card.jsx:14`.

## Tool & Library Notes

- 2026-07-14 — **Fuse.js has no TF-IDF/BM25 and no document-frequency term whatsoever** — it is a Bitap approximate-string matcher scoring `fuzziness × key weight × field-length norm`. Do not reach for it expecting relevance ranking; it ranks by *string closeness*, not by *informativeness*. MiniSearch by contrast ships real BM25+ (`k=1.2, b=0.7, d=0.5`) whose IDF uses the Lucene-style `log(1 + …)` guard, so the classic negative-IDF pathology does **not** bite at small corpus sizes (~29 docs). Sources: fusejs.io scoring-theory docs; `lucaong/minisearch/src/MiniSearch.ts`.
- 2026-07-14 — Known MiniSearch caveat (open issue `lucaong/minisearch#129`): field-length normalization (`fieldLength / averageFieldLength`) can **dominate IDF**, letting a very short document outrank a longer, more relevant one. This bites this codebase specifically because artifact titles are short and descriptions are long — field boosts must be tuned and regression-tested, not left at defaults.
